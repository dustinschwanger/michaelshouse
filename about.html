About Us

Our Mission and History

Some of the most difficult criminal cases to investigate and prosecute are those involving the physical and/or sexual abuse of children, sometimes as young as two or three, because many times the only witnesses are the victim and the perpetrator. Therefore, one of the key pieces of evidence in a child sexual abuse case is the initial interview of the child victim.

It is vital that these initial interviews be conducted by investigators who are trained in forensic interviewing techniques so that the integrity of any disclosures by the child can be maintained. Often, perpetrators will attempt to minimize, deny, or otherwise attempt to discredit the child's statements, thus necessitating the need for interviews to be conducted using accepted forensic methods. Moreover, to limit trauma to a child victim, it is always preferable to conduct only one appropriately formatted interview.

To this end, Michael's House, established in honor of child victim Michael Wasserman, was opened in 2005. Michael's House provides a child-friendly environment in which these sensitive interviews can be conducted and recorded with elaborate video equipment. In the past, children were typically interviewed in the cold and sterile atmosphere of a police precinct and be exposed to surrounding that did nothing but amplify the horrible experience of being a child victim of abuse. In contrast, through forensic interviews at Michael's House, children are more at ease in discussing abuse that may have occurred.

Goals

  • Minimize the trauma these crimes and the subsequent court proceedings inflict on the child victim and family members in their support group.
  • Increase the likelihood of conviction of perpetrators for these offenses.
  • Provide support for the child victims and family members in their support group through immediate follow-up professional counseling and medical services.
  • Ensure that perpetrators face realistic consequences for their crimes, together with mandatory treatment when appropriate.
  • Encourage and promote prompt resolution of these cases, giving due consideration to the rights of the accused.

A Vision Becomes a Reality

2002

Erie County was the recipient of a grant from the Ohio Attorney General to receive funding to establish a subpoena.

2004

A SANE (Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner) was established to conduct pediatric assessments of victims.

2004

A federal grant was awarded in the amount of $25,000 from the National Children's Alliance.

2005

In January, property is secured at 203 West Madison Street and will eventually become the site of Michael's House.

2005

Friends of Michael's House was established as a non-profit board of community members.

2017

Michael's House earned National Accreditation from the National Children's Alliance.

Today

Michael's House serves more than 100 victims annually.
